# Card Sort: PMM OS Research Workspace

Method: **closed sort** (validating the Phase 5 draft IA), run with the user as participant.

## The user's grouping (their words)

The user chose a **two-zone mental model** — *Research* vs *Strategy* — but pulled two things to
the top for prominence: the **GTM readout** (it's what you present) and the **Databases** (cross-cutting
slicing deserves its own home). Desks are just **"Desks,"** not "Research desks." Reconciled menu:

### Overview
- Home / exec summary + project health

### GTM readout  *(own top-level destination — answer-first, prominent)*
- GTM readout → Present mode

### — RESEARCH — *(zone)*
- **Desks** *(renamed from "Research desks")*
  - Customer, Competitive, Market, Pricing, Channels, KOL, Events, GTM, Product → each → its findings
- **Databases** *(own prominent home in this zone)*
  - Findings · Evidence / Sources · Events

### — STRATEGY — *(zone)*
- Positioning
- Messaging
- Narrative
- Campaign
- Coach review

## Singletons / unsure
- **GTM readout** — conceptually a Strategy output, but the user promoted it to a standalone top
  destination so it's reachable in one click for presenting. Secondary home: the Strategy zone.
- **Databases** — chosen as a top-level section *and* the user picked the two-zone model; reconciled
  as a prominent item **within the Research zone** (it's research data), not a third zone.

## What changed from the draft sitemap (the valuable findings)
1. **From 5 flat sections → two labeled zones (Research · Strategy).** Phase 5 listed Overview ·
   Research desks · Strategy · GTM readout · Databases as peers. The user thinks in two *zones* with
   grouped items under each — a Notion-style sidebar with section headers.
2. **GTM readout jumps to the top**, right after Overview — an *answer-first* menu (the recommendation
   sits above the research that justifies it), mirroring the Minto structure of the readout itself.
3. **"Research desks" → "Desks."** The whole workspace is research, so the qualifier is redundant.
4. **Databases stay prominent** (a named item in the Research zone), not buried inside individual desks
   — the cross-desk slicing (sourced/unsourced, events by area/quarter) is a first-class job.

`Overview` · `GTM readout` · **Research** [ Desks · Databases ] · **Strategy** [ Positioning ·
Messaging · Narrative · Campaign · Coach review ]

Two zones, the two answer-first destinations (Overview, GTM readout) on top, 3 levels deep.
