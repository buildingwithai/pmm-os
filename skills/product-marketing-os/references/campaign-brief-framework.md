# Campaign Brief Framework

Use this framework when the user needs a complete campaign brief, not just a launch plan. A launch plan coordinates a release. A campaign brief defines the strategic campaign that turns a message into market action.

## Six-step guided flow

1. Setup
   - Campaign name, campaign type, full brief or light brief.

2. Strategy
   - Background, context, uploaded/source documents, SMART objective, audience.

3. Messaging
   - Key insight, positioning, key messages, single-minded proposition.

4. Execution
   - Channel plan, deliverables, timeline, budget allocation.

5. Governance
   - KPIs, RACI, approvals, owners.

6. Review and export
   - Quality check, gaps, revision plan, export-ready formats.

## 14-section campaign brief

1. Campaign Name
2. Background and Context
3. Objective in SMART format
4. Target Audience
5. Key Insight
6. Positioning Statement
7. Key Messages
8. Single-Minded Proposition
9. Channel Plan
10. Content Deliverables
11. Timeline
12. Budget
13. Success Metrics and KPIs
14. RACI and Approvals

## Common mistakes to catch

- Too long or unfocused
- More than one primary objective
- Vague audience
- No human insight
- SMP tries to say two things
- No measurable success metrics
- No competitive context
- Missing why now
- No owner or approval path

## Campaign templates

| Template | Use when | Typical depth |
|---|---|---|
| Product Launch | New product or major feature | Full brief |
| Feature Update | Existing product improvement | Light or full brief |
| Competitive Response | Competitor move requires market defense | Full brief, accelerated |
| Seasonal Campaign | Time-bound market moment | Full brief |
| Re-engagement | Dormant user activation | Light or full brief |
| Brand Awareness | Top-of-funnel market education | Full brief |

## Quality gate

A campaign brief is not ready unless it has one primary audience, one primary objective, one single-minded proposition, a channel plan with owners, and a measurement plan with targets.
