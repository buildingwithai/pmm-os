# Messaging — Plotline *(gold-standard example)*

*Sets the bar for `pmm-messaging-positioning` and `pmm-messaging-hierarchy`. Built
on the positioning (01). Every claim carries proof or a named gap; persona
variants genuinely differ.*

## Message house

**Primary claim (the one thing):**
> Get the answer yourself, in minutes — without SQL or the data queue.

**Pillar 1 — Ask in plain English.**
- *Claim:* Type the question, get the analysis. No query language, no pre-built
  report to hunt for.
- *Proof:* live demo — "where do trial users drop off?" → funnel in ~10s; 71% of
  weekly active users are non-technical.

**Pillar 2 — No tickets, no waiting.**
- *Claim:* Auto-instrumentation + self-serve means you're not blocked on
  engineering to add an event or on a data analyst to run the query.
- *Proof:* median time-to-first-answer 4 min vs. ~2-day queue benchmark.

**Pillar 3 — One set of numbers.**
- *Claim:* Shared metric definitions, so product, marketing, and execs use the
  same "active" and "activated."
- *Proof:* definition-of-done is a shared metric catalog; *(gap: quantify "fewer
  reconciliation arguments" — needs a customer quote).*

## Value propositions (claim · who · proof · pain removed)

1. **"Answer your own product questions in minutes."** · PM/growth owner · 4-min
   median first answer · removes the multi-day data-queue wait.
2. **"Analytics your non-technical team will actually use."** · PMM / lifecycle ·
   71% non-technical WAU · removes the "we bought a tool and nobody opened it"
   shelfware risk.
3. **"Stop arguing about whose number is right."** · Head of Product / exec ·
   shared metric catalog · removes cross-team number reconciliation. *(proof gap:
   customer testimonial.)*

## Persona variants (note how the emphasis actually changes)

- **PM / growth owner (user):** speed + independence. *"Stop filing tickets to ask
  why trial users churn. Ask Plotline, get the funnel, ship the fix this week."*
- **PMM / lifecycle (user):** adoption + shared numbers. *"Build cohorts and
  measure campaigns against the same definition of 'activated' product uses — no
  SQL, no data-team queue."*
- **Head of Product (economic buyer):** team throughput + no shelfware. *"Your
  team makes more decisions per week, on numbers everyone trusts, without adding a
  data hire."*
- **Head of Data, if present (potential blocker):** reframe, don't fight. *"Plotline
  handles the self-serve 'why' questions so your queue is freed for the modeling
  work only your team can do."*

## Objections this messaging invites + the reframe

1. *"We already have Vista."* → "And how many people on your team actually open
   it? Plotline is the one the PMs use without you." (lean on shelfware.)
2. *"Plain-English analytics can't be trusted."* → show the generated query +
   definition; it's transparent, not a black box.
3. *"We'll just ask our analyst."* → "How long is that queue right now?" (speed +
   freeing the analyst for real modeling.)

## Channel-specific (genuinely adapted, not the pitch pasted 3×)

- **Homepage hero:** *"Product analytics for teams who don't write SQL. Ask a
  question, get the answer — in minutes, not a ticket queue."*
- **Sales one-liner:** *"Plotline lets your PMs and marketers answer their own
  product-data questions in minutes, so decisions stop waiting on the data team."*
- **Cold-email opener:** *"How long does it take someone on your product team to
  get an answer to 'why did activation drop last week'? At most teams your size
  it's a multi-day ticket. Plotline makes it four minutes."*
