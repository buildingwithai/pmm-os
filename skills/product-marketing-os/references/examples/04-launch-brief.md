# Launch Brief — Plotline "Ask" (plain-English questions) *(gold-standard example)*

*Sets the bar for `pmm-launch-brief` and `pmm-go-to-market`. Tiered, with owners,
timeline, metrics, and risks — sized to the launch, not a generic checklist.*

## What's launching & launch tier

**Plotline Ask** — the plain-English question box (type a question, get a funnel/
cohort/chart). This is the feature that *is* the positioning, so:

**Tier 1 (marquee).** Rationale: it's our core differentiator vs. Vista/Pulse and
the reason a non-technical buyer chooses us. A Tier-1 launch (not a changelog
line) is warranted because it changes the category story, not just the feature
set.

## Audience & narrative

- **Primary:** PM/growth/PMM owners at 20–200-person B2B SaaS without a data team.
- **Narrative angle (the before/after):** *Before — you file a ticket and wait
  days to learn why activation dropped. After — you ask Plotline and know in four
  minutes.* The unlock, not the mechanism.

## Messaging for the launch (from 02-messaging)

- Hero: *"Ask your product data a question. Get the answer in minutes — no SQL."*
- Proof in the launch: the 4-minute time-to-first-answer stat + the live demo.

## Success metrics (leading → lagging)

- **Leading (week 1):** % of active accounts that run ≥1 Ask query; demo→trial CVR
  on the launch page; launch-email CTR.
- **Mid (30 day):** activation rate of new trials that used Ask vs. that didn't;
  % non-technical users running queries.
- **Lagging (90 day):** trial→paid conversion delta; influence on win rate vs.
  Vista in deals where Ask was demoed.
- **Guardrail:** Ask query accuracy / "this didn't answer my question" rate — kill
  or fix if it spikes.

## Risks & mitigations

- **Risk:** plain-English answers feel like a black box / get distrusted. →
  *Mitigation:* always show the generated query + metric definition; lead the demo
  with transparency.
- **Risk:** sets the expectation of "AI that answers anything," then misses on
  edge questions. → *Mitigation:* scope the launch claim to the common question
  types; publish what it does and doesn't do.
- **Risk:** data-team buyers feel threatened and block. → *Mitigation:* the
  "frees your queue for real modeling" reframe in enablement.

## Approvals & go/no-go

- Go requires: accuracy guardrail green in beta, demo reproducible, sales trained.
- Sign-off: Head of Product (feature), PMM (story), Founder (Tier-1 marquee).
