# Battlecard — Plotline vs. Vista Analytics *(gold-standard example)*

*Sets the bar for `pmm-battlecard` and `pmm-competitive-intelligence`. Sales-ready:
specific, honest about where we lose, with talk tracks a rep can actually say.
Vista is a fictional enterprise incumbent.*

## TL;DR (the 10-second version for a rep)

Vista is the powerful enterprise suite. We win when **the people who need answers
can't write SQL and the data team is a bottleneck** — Vista becomes shelfware
there. We lose when there's a **mature data team that wants warehouse-native
modeling**. Don't fight Vista on analytical depth; fight on *who can actually use
it*.

## Where we win (and the proof)

| We win when… | Why | Proof to cite |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Buyer has no/overloaded data team | Vista needs a maintainer; we don't | 4-min median first answer; 71% non-technical WAU |
| Vista is already shelfware there | Adoption, not features, is their failure | Ask: "how many seats actually log in weekly?" |
| Non-technical owner is the user | Plain-English questions vs. SQL/report-building | Live no-SQL demo |
| Speed of a single answer matters | Self-serve vs. ticket queue | Time-to-answer story |

## Where Vista wins (say it — credibility)

- **Mature data orgs** wanting deep, warehouse-native modeling and governance at
  enterprise scale. *Honest move: disqualify, don't oversell.*
- **Procurement checkbox depth** (long feature RFPs). We counter with adoption +
  time-to-value, not feature parity.

## Objection handling

- *"Vista is the market leader / safer choice."* → "For a team with a data
  department, sure. For yours, the risk isn't the vendor — it's buying powerful
  software your team never opens. That's the failure mode we're built against."
- *"Can Plotline do <deep Vista feature>?"* → "For the 80% of questions your PMs
  actually ask, yes, themselves, in minutes. For the deep-modeling 20%, that's
  what a warehouse + your data team is for — we don't pretend to replace it."
- *"We'd have to re-instrument."* → auto-instrumentation means days, not a
  re-tagging project; offer a migration/parallel-run.

## Discovery questions (qualify in or out)

1. "Who on your team can answer a product-data question today without help?"
2. "How long does a typical 'why did X change' question take to get answered?"
3. "Do you have a data team, and what's their queue look like?"
4. "When you last bought an analytics tool, what % of the team actually adopted it?"

## Traps to avoid

- **Don't** get pulled into a feature-by-feature matrix vs. Vista — that's their
  home field. Reframe to adoption + time-to-value.
- **Don't** claim we replace a data warehouse. We don't; saying so loses the
  technical evaluator's trust.

## How to win the deal

Run a **time-boxed proof**: pick one real question the buyer's team has been
waiting on, answer it live in Plotline in the call. The contrast with their
current queue *is* the pitch.
