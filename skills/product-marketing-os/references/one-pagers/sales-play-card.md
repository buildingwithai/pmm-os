# Sales Play Card — word-for-word language, findable in 10 seconds

The Force Management / Highspot Plays standard five-field format: a rep should never leave the
deal record to reach it. A library without the 90-day pruning rule becomes a graveyard.

- **When to use:** a repeatable selling moment needs a script — a competitor trigger, a persona
  objection, a stage transition (feeds pmm-sales-narrative and the battlecard's talk tracks).
- **Length rule:** one page per play; findable in under 10 seconds.
- **Lives where:** Highspot/Seismic/Showpad/HubSpot Playbooks, embedded in the Salesforce/HubSpot
  opportunity view and auto-surfaced by stage + persona. Governance is "enablement owns, sales
  updates": every play has a named owner and the library kills one stale play every 90 days.
- **Hydrating desks:** customer (winning call clips, buyer language) · gtm (stage/signal
  triggers) · product (what to show) · competitive (competitor-triggered variants).

## The six sections are fixed — the ghost generates against them in order

1. Objective — one sentence: what this play accomplishes
2. When to run it — trigger conditions: deal stage, persona, signal
3. Script — word-for-word language with 2–3 variants (what to know/say/show/do)
4. Examples — 2 winning call clips (Gong) + 1 closed-won email thread
5. Red flags — what to watch for, when to escalate or kill the play
6. Follow-up content to send after the call

## Sayable scripts proved by real calls are what earn a play its slot

- Word-for-word sayable scripts, not talking-point abstractions — a rep reads it aloud and it
  sounds like a person.
- Proof by real call recordings rather than hypotheticals; the clips ARE the training.
- Trigger-based surfacing at the moment of need, and win-rate attribution per play.
- One stale play dies every 90 days — pruning is what keeps the library trusted.

## The skeleton — trigger, script, proof, kill-switch

```markdown
# Play: [governing thought — e.g. "when [competitor] enters at stage 2, reframe on total cost of downtime"]
*Owner: [enablement name] · Last verified: [date] · Win rate when run: [x% vs y% baseline] — Source: [CRM/Gong, date]*

**Objective (one sentence):** [what this play accomplishes — e.g. "move the eval criterion from
list price to time-to-recovery before the demo"]

**When to run it:** stage: [stage] · persona: [persona] · signal: [trigger — e.g. "[competitor]
tagged on the opp" / "prospect says '[phrase]'"]

**Script (what to know / say / show / do):**
- Know: [the 1–2 facts to have loaded] — Source: [battlecard/positioning page]
- Say (variant A — [persona/context]): "[word-for-word sayable language]"
- Say (variant B — [persona/context]): "[word-for-word variant]"
- Show: [the asset/demo moment]
- Do: [the next action — book X, send Y, tag Z]

**Examples (real, not hypothetical):**
- Winning call clip: [Gong link + 1-line why it worked] — [date]
- Winning call clip: [Gong link + why] — [date]
- Closed-won email thread: [link] — [date]

**Red flags:** [what to watch for — e.g. "prospect keeps returning to per-seat price ⇒ economic
buyer isn't in the room"] · escalate to [role] when [condition] · kill the play if [condition]

**Follow-up content:** [asset to send after the call + the one-line send note]

**Ask:** [Verb-first — e.g. "Publish this play into the [stage]/[persona] surface rule by [date]
— owner: [enablement]; review at the 90-day prune on [date]."]
```
