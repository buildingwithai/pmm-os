# Content Strategy One-Pager — the minimum viable strategy the team says NO with

One page the content lead defends in planning: when new requests arrive, they're triaged against
it. Its main power is what it lets the team decline. Anything longer becomes a doc nobody
re-reads.

- **When to use:** setting or resetting content strategy for a quarter; before building the
  editorial calendar (which lives separately and derives from this page).
- **Length rule:** one page; 3–4 pillars maximum (more breaks consistency); a cadence you can
  sustain 6 months; per-pillar metrics.
- **Lives where:** wiki page the content lead defends in planning ("show the plan with impact
  forecasts"); quarterly pillar review; feeds the editorial calendar.
- **Hydrating desks:** customer (pains in their own words, from calls/win-loss) · channels
  (channel scoring, cadence) · gtm (pipeline KPI) · analyst-influencer (distribution/repurposing
  targets).

## The eight sections are fixed — the ghost generates against them in order

1. North Star KPI + content's job this quarter ("drive paid signups", not "awareness")
2. Audience: ICP + top 3–5 pains in their own words (from sales calls / win-loss)
3. Positioning promise: for who / we help achieve what / proved by which evidence
4. 3–4 content pillars max, each mapped to an ICP + funnel stage + 1 main metric + 1–2 early
   signals
5. Channel priority: 1–2 power channels, scored on audience fit and capacity
6. Cadence table: channel × frequency × format × owner (set at ~70% of full capacity)
7. Distribution/repurposing loop
8. 90-day targets + first 30-day calendar

## One job and one pipeline KPI per content type is the discipline

- Every content type has exactly one job and one KPI tied to pipeline, not vanity metrics.
- Pillars are sourced from real buyer language (sales calls, win-loss), not brainstorms.
- A realistic cadence beats an ambitious one — set it at ~70% of capacity.
- The page's main power is what it lets the team say NO to.
