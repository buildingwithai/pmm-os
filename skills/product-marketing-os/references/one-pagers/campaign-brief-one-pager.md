# Campaign Brief — one page, one message, signed off before any asset exists

"One-page briefs get read, ten-page briefs get skimmed." The brief is the kickoff doc and the
mid-campaign scope-creep referee — sign-off on the brief is cheaper than rewriting finished
creative.

- **When to use:** any campaign before production starts (pmm-campaign-brief output). The full
  14-section flow lives in [campaign-brief-framework.md](../campaign-brief-framework.md); this is
  its one-page render.
- **Length rule:** one page for most campaigns, two pages absolute max; big multi-channel launches
  stretch to 3–5 pages. Research decks and brand guidelines go in an appendix link, never inline.
- **Lives where:** wiki or work-management tool (Notion/Asana/Confluence); signed off by
  stakeholders BEFORE any asset is produced.
- **Hydrating desks:** customer (audience, insight) · channels (channel table) · market
  (context) · gtm (funnel role, metrics).

## The ten sections are fixed — the ghost generates against them in order

1. Campaign name + single owner (a person, not a committee)
2. Objective: one measurable sentence tied to a business metric
3. Target audience — specific enough to almost call by name
4. Single key message (≤15 words) + tone / copy direction
5. Channel table: channel, funnel role, tactic, budget share, owner
6. Assets & deliverables
7. Timeline & milestones
8. Budget (with contingency and unlock rule)
9. Success metrics as pre-agreed numbers ("500 trials, 50 upgrades, 3% CTR")
10. Mandatories / approval flow / risks

## Problem-first and ONE message is what separates campaigns from noise

- Problem-first, not deliverable-first: "lift homepage conversion from 1.2% to 2.5%" beats "we
  need a new homepage".
- The brief commits to ONE message — campaigns that say three things say nothing.
- Honest budget with a named contingency unlock rule.
- Numeric success criteria agreed up front are what let the campaign be evaluated honestly
  afterward.

## The skeleton — objective as a number, message under 15 words

```markdown
# [Campaign name]: [governing thought — e.g. "lift trial starts from 400 to 550/mo in [segment] by [date]"]
*Owner: [one person, not a committee] · Signed off by: [names] on [date] · Status: [draft/approved]*

**Objective (one measurable sentence):** [move [metric] from [baseline] to [target] by [date]]
— Baseline source: [analytics, date]

**Target audience:** [specific enough to almost call by name — role, company type, trigger,
current behavior] — Source: [ICP/persona page]

**Single key message (≤15 words):**
> [the one message]

Tone / copy direction: [3 adjectives + one banned register]

**Channel table:**
| Channel | Funnel role | Tactic | Budget share | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [channel] | [TOFU/MOFU/BOFU] | [tactic] | [n%] | [name] |

**Assets & deliverables:** [asset — owner — due date] · [asset — owner — due date]

**Timeline & milestones:** brief sign-off [date] → assets final [date] → launch [date] →
mid-flight check [date] → wrap + readout [date]

**Budget:** $[n] total · contingency $[n] · unlock rule: [condition + approver]

**Success metrics (pre-agreed numbers):** [e.g. "500 trials · 50 upgrades · 3% CTR"] —
Baseline sources: [analytics/CRM, date]

**Mandatories / approvals / risks:** mandatories: [legal lines, brand rules] · approval flow:
[who, in what order, SLA] · risks: [risk → mitigation + owner]

**Ask:** [Verb-first — e.g. "Sign off this brief by [date] so production starts [date] — owner:
[name]. No asset is produced before sign-off."]
```
