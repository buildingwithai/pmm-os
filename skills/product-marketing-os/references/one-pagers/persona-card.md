# Persona Card — one page of buying insight per persona

The card a rep references before every first call. 80% of the space goes to buying insight, 20% to
bio — zero space on hobbies and ages ("demographic fanfiction" changes no copy and wins no deals).

- **When to use:** building or refreshing buyer personas (pmm-personas / pmm-customer-research
  output); 2–4 personas max — more means you're not segmenting.
- **Length rule:** one page per persona; 80% buying insight / 20% bio; reps should reference it
  before every first call.
- **Lives where:** wiki page plus a one-page cheat-sheet version pushed into sales onboarding and
  enablement tools — "a one-page cheat sheet beats a 20-slide deck every time"; each persona maps
  to specific sequences, content, and talk tracks.
- **Hydrating desks:** customer (calls, quotes, objections, 5 Rings) · competitive (alternatives
  they rejected) · channels (info sources and channels they trust).

## The nine sections are fixed — the ghost generates against them in order

1. Header: name, title, buying-committee role (champion/economic buyer/blocker/user) + authority
   level (can sign / recommend / veto)
2. One compiled verbatim quote capturing their voice
3. Priority initiative: what triggered them to evaluate now
4. Success factors: what winning looks like to them (their outcomes, not your features)
5. Perceived barriers + top 3 objections (from real call recordings)
6. Decision criteria / evaluation scorecard
7. Info sources and channels
8. JTBD + alternatives they rejected
9. Exclusion: who this persona is NOT / disqualification signals

## Behavioral fields that change copy beat demographics that change nothing

- Trigger, objections, and decision levers are the fields that rewrite sequences and landing
  pages; age and hobbies rewrite nothing.
- Objections come from call recordings, not assumptions — Revella's 5 Rings (initiatives, success
  factors, barriers, criteria, journey) is the spine.
- Real interview quotes carry the credibility; a quarterly refresh keeps it alive.
- It gets ignored when it's a Canva poster of an imaginary person.

## The skeleton — one card per persona, insight over bio

```markdown
# [Persona name]: the [buying-committee role] who [governing thought — e.g. "signs the deal but only after security clears it"]
*Last reviewed: [date] · Owner: [name] · Built from [N] calls/interviews — Source: [Gong tag / study, date]*

**Header:** [name] · [title] · [champion / economic buyer / blocker / user] ·
authority: [can sign / recommend / veto]

> "[One compiled verbatim quote in their voice — from a real call]" — Source: [call, date]

**Priority initiative (why they're evaluating NOW):** [the trigger — e.g. "board asked for an
incident-cost number after the [month] outage"] — Source: [call/win-loss, date]

**Success factors (their outcomes, not our features):**
- [what winning looks like to them, in their words]
- [second outcome]

**Perceived barriers + top 3 objections (from real recordings):**
1. "[objection verbatim]" — Source: [call, date]
2. "[objection verbatim]" — Source: [call, date]
3. "[objection verbatim]" — Source: [call, date]

**Decision criteria / evaluation scorecard:** [what they score vendors on, ranked — e.g.
"time-to-value < 2 wks · SSO/SOC 2 · price per seat vs incumbent"]

**Info sources and channels:** [where they actually learn — communities, newsletters, peers,
analysts] — Source: [channels desk finding]

**JTBD + alternatives they rejected:** When [situation], I want [motivation], so I can [outcome].
Rejected: [alternative + the reason, from calls] — Source: [call, date]

**This persona is NOT:** [exclusions / disqualification signals — e.g. "not the hands-on admin;
if they ask for API docs first, route to the practitioner persona"]

**Ask:** [Verb-first — e.g. "Adopt this card in first-call prep by [date] — owner: [enablement
lead]; refresh from new calls quarterly."]
```
