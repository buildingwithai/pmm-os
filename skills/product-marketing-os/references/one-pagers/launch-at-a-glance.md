# Launch-at-a-Glance — one page with an owner and a date on every line

The cross-functional alignment page: reviewed in a 15-minute walkthrough with every named owner.
It is a decision document, not a filing exercise — and it dies the day a slipped date goes
un-updated.

- **When to use:** any launch (pmm-launch-brief / pmm-go-to-market output). P1/P2 launches need
  only this brief; P0 gets this page plus a separate full GTM plan. Written 4+ weeks out for P0,
  2–3 for P1, 1 for P2.
- **Length rule:** one page; owners + dates on every line.
- **Lives where:** Notion/Coda/Confluence launch hub (Coda and Pendo ship this as a standard
  template); kicked off in a 45-minute cross-functional review; updated immediately when dates
  slip — stale dates are how briefs die.
- **Hydrating desks:** product (what's launching) · customer (who it's for, personas) · gtm
  (tier, motion, enablement) · channels (channel plan) · events (launch-moment timing) · market
  (opportunity size).

## The eleven sections are fixed — the ghost generates against them in order

1. TL;DR (3–4 lines)
2. What's launching + launch tier (P0/P1/P2) + date + launch owner
3. Who it's for (personas, eligible plans, opportunity size)
4. Why we're launching it (internal motivation, in evidence-based plain sentences)
5. One-line pitch / core message ("[Product] now [does X] so [who] can [outcome] without [pain]")
6. Channel plan: channel, timing, owner, goal — one line each
7. Sales/CS enablement notes: talking points, objections addressed, pipeline deals to re-engage
8. FAQs (pre-answered inbound questions)
9. Risks & mitigation
10. Success metrics + 30-day post-launch review date
11. "Not changing" section + readiness checklist

## Evidence-based reasons and the "not changing" section keep it a decision document

- Reasons are written as evidence, not vibes: "we lost three mid-market deals in 60 days for lack
  of this capability" beats "improve conversion".
- Owners and dates sit on every line — an unowned line is a line that won't happen.
- The "not changing" section prevents re-litigating settled debates in the kickoff.
- Update the page the moment a date slips; stale dates kill the whole brief's authority.

## The skeleton — TL;DR first, owners everywhere

```markdown
# [Launch name]: we ship [what] on [date] because [evidence-based reason]
*Tier: [P0/P1/P2] · Launch owner: [name] · Last updated: [date]*

**TL;DR (3–4 lines):** [what's launching, for whom, the core message, and the one metric that
defines success — readable alone]

**What's launching:** [feature/product] · tier [P0/P1/P2] · GA [date] · owner [name]

**Who it's for:** [personas] · eligible plans: [plans] · opportunity: [number w/ comparator,
e.g. "22% of ARR base requested it, up from 9%"] — Source: [CRM/research, date]

**Why we're launching:** [evidence sentence — e.g. "lost [n] deals in [period] for lack of this
capability"] — Source: [win-loss data, date]

**One-line pitch:** [Product] now [does X] so [who] can [outcome] without [pain]

**Channel plan (one line each):**
| Channel | Timing | Owner | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| [channel] | [date] | [name] | [numeric goal] |

**Sales/CS enablement:** talking points: [link] · objections addressed: [top 2 inline] ·
pipeline deals to re-engage: [n deals, list link] — Source: [CRM filter, date]

**FAQs:** [Q → one-line A] · [Q → A] *(pre-answer the inbound before it arrives)*

**Risks & mitigation:** [risk] → [mitigation + owner] · [risk] → [mitigation + owner]

**Success metrics:** [metric + target w/ comparator] — Source of baseline: [analytics, date] ·
30-day post-launch review: [date, owner]

**NOT changing:** [pricing / plans / API — the settled debates, named] ·
**Readiness checklist:** [docs ✓/✗ · support macro ✓/✗ · billing ✓/✗ · legal ✓/✗]

**Ask:** [Verb-first — e.g. "Confirm your line's owner + date in the 15-min walkthrough on
[date] — all owners named above."]
```
