# Win/Loss Readout — quantified frequency delivered live, never just circulated

The quarterly readout: 10–15 slides backed by one page per theme, presented live — "the CEO who
hears the findings live ships fixes faster than the CEO who scans the deck on a flight." The
accountability loop (did last quarter's fixes move win rate?) is what stops it becoming a data
dump execs learn to skip.

- **When to use:** the quarterly win/loss program readout (routes through
  pmm-competitive-intelligence's win/loss depth); themes flow directly into battlecards, roadmap,
  and messaging. PMM typically owns the program.
- **Length rule:** quarterly deck of 10–15 slides max, backed by one-page-per-theme summaries
  (pattern + quotes + recommendation + owner); delivered as a live 60–90 minute readout, never
  just circulated as a file.
- **Lives where:** presented live in the quarterly business review to sales, product, and
  marketing leadership; theme pages live in the wiki and link into the battlecard/messaging
  refreshes they trigger.
- **Hydrating desks:** customer (interviews, verbatims — the spine) · competitive (competitor
  table) · gtm (win rate by segment/deal size) · product (ranked gap list).

## The seven sections are fixed — the ghost generates against them in order

1. Executive summary: 3–5 recommendations up front
2. Win rate by segment and deal size vs last quarter
3. Top 3 win drivers + top 3 loss drivers, each with frequency data ("gap X in 32% of losses, up
   from 18%") and 3–5 verbatim buyer quotes
4. Competitor table: who appeared in losses, how often, what buyers cited
5. Status of LAST quarter's action items (did they ship, did win rate move)
6. New action items: max 1–2 per owner, with 30-day deadlines
7. Downstream updates triggered: battlecard refreshes, positioning shifts, new talk tracks,
   ranked product-gap list

## Quantified frequency plus verbatims is what makes it decision-ready

- "Buyers mentioned integrations" is not decision-ready; "32% of losses, up from 18%" is — every
  driver carries frequency with a quarter-over-quarter comparator.
- Verbatim quotes carry the credibility the percentages alone can't.
- Reviewing last quarter's commitments — and whether win rate moved — is the accountability loop
  that keeps leadership showing up.
