# GTM Strategy-on-a-Page — falsifiable commitments a board can read in 5 minutes

The executive summary sitting on top of the full 15–30 page GTM doc. "If you can't print it, it's
too vague." The test: it changes what the team does on Monday.

- **When to use:** a GTM motion is being set or reset (pmm-go-to-market / plg-gtm-strategy
  output); exec reviews and board pre-reads; page one of the full GTM strategy.
- **Length rule:** one printable page; readable by a board member in under 5 minutes.
- **Lives where:** exec reviews and board pre-reads; page one of the GTM doc in the wiki
  (Info-Tech sells exactly this as "GTM Strategy on a Page"); referenced in weekly pipeline
  reviews to check incoming deals against ICP and channel assumptions; walked through in
  sales/marketing onboarding.
- **Hydrating desks:** gtm (motion, sales model) · customer (ICP block) · market (category) ·
  channels (the ONE channel) · pricing (posture) · competitive (alternatives) · product (offer
  proof).

## The eight sections are fixed — the ghost generates against them in order

1. ICP block in ~6 lines: segment, roles (signs/uses/blocks), trigger, cost of doing nothing,
   alternatives, non-fit
2. One-sentence offer / value proposition + 3 proof points
3. Positioning & market category (one line)
4. GTM motion: sales-led / PLG / channel / hybrid + sales model
5. Channels: ONE primary + one support (five channels = none)
6. Pricing & packaging posture (one line)
7. 3 numbers: demand metric, conversion metric, margin/pipeline metric
8. Phased timeline / next 90 days with owners

## Decisions, not descriptions, are what make execs use it

- One named channel, a one-sentence offer, pre-committed numeric targets — every line is a
  falsifiable commitment.
- Execs ignore it when it's a mission-statement collage.
- Dependencies locked in order — ICP before messaging before channels — is what makes the summary
  trustworthy.

## The skeleton — one page, every line falsifiable

```markdown
# GTM: win [segment] through [one channel] with [offer] — [demand target] by [date]
*Last reviewed: [date] · Owner: [name] · Full GTM doc: [link]*

**ICP (~6 lines):**
- Segment: [company type, size, vertical]
- Roles: signs [role] · uses [role] · blocks [role]
- Trigger: [the event that starts evaluation]
- Cost of doing nothing: [number w/ comparator] — Source: [research, date]
- Alternatives: [what they do today]
- Non-fit: [who we walk away from]

**Offer (one sentence):** [value proposition]
- Proof: [number w/ comparator] — Source: [link, date]
- Proof: [customer evidence] — Source: [link, date]
- Proof: [third-party validation] — Source: [link, date]

**Positioning & category (one line):** For [X], the [category] that [Y], unlike [Z].

**Motion:** [sales-led / PLG / channel / hybrid] · sales model: [self-serve / inside / field]

**Channels:** primary: [ONE channel + why it wins] · support: [one channel]
*(five channels = none)*

**Pricing posture (one line):** [model + range + packaging stance]

**The 3 numbers:**
| Metric | Target | Baseline | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Demand: [metric] | [target] | [baseline] | [analytics, date] |
| Conversion: [metric] | [target] | [baseline] | [CRM, date] |
| Margin/pipeline: [metric] | [target] | [baseline] | [finance, date] |

**Next 90 days:** [phase 1: milestone, owner, date] → [phase 2: milestone, owner, date] →
[phase 3: milestone, owner, date]

**Ask:** [Verb-first — e.g. "Approve the single-channel bet and the 3 targets at the [date] exec
review — owner: [name]; deals get checked against this ICP in weekly pipeline review."]
```
