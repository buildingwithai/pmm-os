# Persona Matrix Template (Message × Persona)

**Purpose:** Map every pillar to every persona so each buyer hears the version that moves *them* — same house, different door. The matrix is what stops "one-size-fits-all" copy and "every rep invents their own pitch" at the same time.

**When to use:** After the [message house](message-house-template.md) stands and personas exist (2-4 max — more means you're not segmenting). Use it whenever copy is being written for a specific audience: landing page variants, outbound sequences, creator briefs, sales talk tracks.

**Time to complete:** 1-2 hours if personas and pillars already exist. If personas don't exist, stop — build them first (persona library, Five Rings) — a matrix over invented personas is fan fiction with columns.

## Instructions

1. Columns = personas (2-4). Rows = your pillars, then objections, then channels. Every cell answers: *how does this pillar sound to this person?*
2. Write each cell as sayable copy, not a summary. The test: a rep or a creator could read the cell aloud and it would work.
3. Emphasis must genuinely change across a row. If the three cells are the same sentence with the persona name swapped, you've made a decoration, not an adaptation. (Rule of thumb: users get "how," champions get "what + proof," economic buyers get "why + risk.")
4. Every cell keeps its proof. Adaptation changes the framing, never the evidence discipline.
5. Fill the objection row from real calls/threads, not imagination — the objection each persona *actually* raises differs.
6. Mark the beachhead column. Launch copy leads with that persona; the others get variants, not the lead.

## Filled example — Plotline

*Personas from the customer desk (2026-06-12): three buying roles visible in the beta + win/loss corpus. Beachhead: the PM column — she feels the data-queue pain daily and is the self-serve entry point the PLG motion depends on.*

**Personas:** **Priya — the PM** (owns a metric at a 20–200-person B2B SaaS; every "why" question is a ticket) · **Marcus — lifecycle / PMM** (measures campaigns and activation; product and marketing dashboards disagree) · **Dana — Head of Product** (economic buyer; bought Vista, three people opened it last quarter, being asked to fund a data hire)

**Beachhead: Priya** — highest question volume (the wedge's value scales with how often "why" gets asked) and the free tier's natural first user.

| | **Priya — the PM** | **Marcus — lifecycle / PMM** | **Dana — Head of Product** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Situation & trigger** | A metric moved and she needs the why this sprint; "every 'why did this drop' is a ticket" (beta interview #7) is her normal | Campaign wrap-up; product and marketing report different "activated" numbers and the QBR turns into an argument | Renewal on a suite nobody opens; the alternative on the table is a data hire |
| **Pillar 1: Ask in plain English** | "Type 'where do trial users drop off before inviting a teammate?' — the funnel comes back in four minutes, query shown. Ship the fix this week." · Proof: live ask-to-answer demo | "Ask 'which campaign's signups activate best?' yourself — no SQL, no favor owed to the data team." · Proof: 71% of active users write no SQL | "Analytics your team actually opens — because anyone can use it, not just the two people who can query." · Proof: beta adoption telemetry |
| **Pillar 2: No tickets, no waiting** | "The question you'd have parked in the queue gets answered the same afternoon — decisions stop waiting on someone else's sprint." · Proof: ~2-day queue → 4-min median | "Mid-campaign, you check activation daily — not once, two days after you filed the ticket." · Proof: auto-instrumented events, no eng ticket | "More decisions per week without adding a data hire — the queue disappears instead of growing." · Proof: win/loss register ("by the time it comes back, the sprint's planned") |
| **Pillar 3: One set of numbers** | "Your funnel uses the same 'activated' as everyone else's — no relitigating definitions in the review." · Proof: shared metric catalog demo | "Measure every campaign against the same definition of 'activated' — the whose-number-is-right argument ends." · Proof: catalog-enforced definitions in every answer | "One catalog, every team, numbers that reconcile — trust in the numbers is the ROI." · Proof: definition + generated query shown under every answer |
| **Top objection (verbatim)** | "Plain-English analytics can't be trusted." → "Every answer shows the generated query and the metric definition. Check it — that's the point." | "We already have dashboards." → "Keep them — dashboards show what happened. Plotline answers the follow-up why, without the next ticket." | "We already pay for Vista." → "And how many of your team actually open it? Seats are unlimited here; you pay for usage, not shelfware." |
| **Where they are (channel)** | r/ProductManagement + Lenny's podcast; native format: genuine tactic post ("how do you get answers without a data team?" — 412↑ anchor thread), not an ad | Growth/lifecycle newsletters + Reforge community; format: a teardown of a real whose-number-is-right reconciliation | Exec peer communities + the pricing page; format: the cost-of-the-queue table (~2 days vs 4 minutes), not a feature tour |
| **First CTA** | Start free (10k MTU) — ask your first question today; the first answer is the hook | Start free; define "activated" once in the catalog and invite product to use the same number | See the cost-of-the-queue math; Business adds SSO and roles when you standardize |

**Exclusions:** **Sam — Head of Data** — a blocker to convert, not a buyer column; brief him separately ("Plotline takes the self-serve 'why' questions off your queue so your team does the modeling only they can"), never message around him. Also anti-ICP by design: data-mature orgs wanting warehouse-native modeling, and pre-PMF teams with no usage yet — selling to them creates churn and a "this isn't a modeling layer" review; disqualify, don't chase.
