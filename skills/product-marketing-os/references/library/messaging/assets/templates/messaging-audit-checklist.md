# Messaging Audit Checklist

**Purpose:** Score existing messaging against the house bar — coverage, consistency, specificity, proof, honesty, freshness — so you know exactly which row to fix first instead of "refreshing the website" on vibes.

**When to use:** Quarterly (the message-house review cadence); before any launch that reuses existing copy; when sales says "the website doesn't say what I say"; when a new PMM inherits a messaging estate; after a competitor repositions.

**Time to complete:** 2-3 hours for a product's core surfaces (homepage, one deck, one sequence, one ad set).

**Scoring:** 0-3 per row, mirroring the [deliverable scorecard](../../../../deliverable-standard.md#4-the-deliverable-scorecard-03-per-row-shippable--2433-no-row-below-2): 0 = absent · 1 = present but broken · 2 = works with gaps · 3 = the bar. **Healthy ≥ 20/27 with no row below 2.** Fix the weakest row first; never "refresh everything."

## Instructions

1. Collect the surfaces before judging any of them: homepage, top landing pages, the current sales deck, the top outbound sequence, the ad copy in market, the app store / marketplace listing. Screenshot with dates.
2. Pull the reference artifacts: the [messaging canvas](messaging-canvas-template.md) and [message house](message-house-template.md). If they don't exist, the audit is already answered — row 1 scores 0; build them first.
3. Score each row with evidence, not impressions. Every score cites the surface and the line that earned it.
4. Run the competitor-swap test literally: paste a rival's name into your headline. Do it in writing, in the audit.
5. End with the fix list: weakest row first, one owner and one date per fix — never "align on messaging."

## Filled example — Plotline draft launch copy, audited pre-launch

*Surfaces: draft homepage hero, draft launch blog, draft Product Hunt listing, draft lifecycle email, draft r/ProductManagement post. Reference: Plotline canvas + house (2026-06-12).*

**Row 1 — source of truth: 3/3.** Canvas + house exist, owned by founding PMM, dated 2026-06-12, roof is one 14-word sentence.

**Row 2 — consistency: 2/3.** Homepage and launch blog share the roof. The draft lifecycle email leads with "beautiful dashboards for your whole team" — "dashboards" is the word the house explicitly demotes (that's Pulse's category, the "what" not the "why"). Fix: email leads with Ask; dashboards mentioned only as "saved answers."

**Row 3 — specificity: 3/3.** Swap test written out: *"Vista answers your product questions in plain English in four minutes"* → **false** — Vista's own homepage leads "The complete analytics platform for data-driven enterprises," analyst-first, weeks-long implementation. The claim names the mechanism (plain English, four minutes, query shown).

**Row 4 — proof on surface: 2/3.** House pillars each carry 3-4 typed proofs, but the draft homepage shows zero proof below the hero — no beta stat, no ask-to-answer recording. Fix: the ask-to-answer screen recording (generated query visible) is the strongest owned proof; put it directly under the hero.

**Row 5 — customer language: 3/3.** Pillar 2 is built on the ICP's literal register ("everything is a ticket" — beta interview #7; "by the time it comes back, the sprint's planned" — win/loss call). The customer is the subject throughout ("ask your product a question"), not the product.

**Row 6 — honesty: 1/3 — the launch blocker.** The draft Product Hunt listing says *"the only analytics tool that answers plain-English questions."* The rival AI-feature sweep (Vista's Copilot beta, Pulse's Insights AI, the incoming LLM-analytics startups) has NOT run — Pulse's Insights AI docs page 404'd, so "only" is confirmed against nothing but today's shipped products. Also: the hero implies "ask anything," but accuracy is verified only for funnels, cohorts, retention, and segmentation; and the "fewer reconciliation arguments" gain is still inferred (no customer quote quantifying it). Fix: strip "only" from every surface until the sweep is in hand; scope the accuracy claim to the common question types; lead with the demonstrable ("get the answer in four minutes, query shown") meanwhile.

**Row 7 — personas: 2/3.** Matrix exists (Priya/Marcus/Dana) and the launch blog leads with Priya. But the r/ProductManagement draft reuses Dana's cost-of-the-queue ROI framing on Priya's channel — PMs in that thread want the how, not the budget case. Fix: swap in Priya's ship-the-fix-this-week framing.

**Row 8 — channel-native: 3/3.** The Reddit draft is a genuine "how we stopped filing analytics tickets" tactic post riding the 412-upvote thread format, not an ad read; the lifecycle email carries one pillar + one CTA (start free, ask your first question); every surface ends in a next step.

**Row 9 — tested: 1/3.** Internal consistency test passed; zero external sessions run. Fix: 6 sessions with the PM beachhead per the [testing script](message-testing-script.md) before copy locks.

**Verdict: 20/27 — but NOT healthy: rows 6 and 9 sit below 2.** The total is a distraction here; the no-row-below-2 rule is doing its job.

| Fix (weakest first) | Owner | Date |
|---|---|---|
| Row 6: strip "only" claims; run rival AI-feature sweep; scope the accuracy claim; get the reconciliation quote | Founding PMM | +1 week, gates launch |
| Row 9: 6 beachhead test sessions | PMM | +2 weeks |
| Row 2: rewrite lifecycle email off "dashboards" | PMM | +3 days |
| Row 4: ask-to-answer recording under hero | Design | +1 week |
| Row 7: Priya-native Reddit draft | PMM | +3 days |
