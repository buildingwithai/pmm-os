# Message Testing Script (Interview Guide)

**Purpose:** A 20-25 minute interview protocol that tells you whether your messaging lands *before* it ships — comprehension, differentiation, emotion, and intent, scored on the same five pillars the `pmm-message-market-fit` skill uses (ICP alignment · differentiation · clarity · emotion · actionability).

**When to use:** After the [message house](message-house-template.md) passes the internal consistency test and before launch copy locks. Also whenever stakeholders are debating two message directions — a week of these interviews ends debates that months of opinion don't.

**Who and how many:** 5-8 participants **per persona column** of your [persona matrix](persona-matrix-template.md) (score per persona, never in aggregate — a 4.0 average hiding a 2.5 with your beachhead is a fail). Recruit real ICP members, not colleagues.

**The stimuli:** 3-6 message cards — the roof line, each pillar line, and 1-2 competitor lines mixed in blind (the competitor lines are your differentiation control).

## Instructions

1. Prepare stimulus cards: your roof + pillars, plus real competitor copy pulled verbatim from their homepages. Same visual format for all — no branding, no design cues.
2. Never pitch. You are testing the words, not selling the product. When a participant asks "so what does it do?", the answer is "what do *you* think it does, from this?"
3. Ask for playback before opinion. "What would you tell a friend this does?" beats "do you like it?" — likability is the weakest signal in messaging research.
4. Score every stimulus per participant on the five pillars (1-5) immediately after each session, while the verbatims are fresh. Capture exact phrases — they are your next iteration's copy.
5. Thresholds: **ship** at ≥4.0 average with no pillar below 3.0 for the beachhead persona; **iterate** at 3.0-4.0; **back to positioning** below 3.0 on ICP alignment or differentiation (that's not a copy problem).
6. Feed results into `pmm-message-market-fit` for the scored specificity report and the four-variant test plan.

## The Script

### 0. Screener (before the session)

- [ ] Matches a named persona: [criteria from the persona matrix]
- [ ] Currently in-market or in-problem (within the last [X] months)
- [ ] Not a friend of the company, not a marketer

### 1. Warm-up — their current state (5 min)

> Establishes the pain baseline in their words BEFORE they see any message. Their vocabulary here is the grading key for "ICP alignment."

1. "Walk me through the last time you [did the job the product addresses]. What did that look like?"
2. "What's the most frustrating part of that today?"
3. "What have you tried? What do you use now?" *(their real alternatives — check against your positioning canvas)*

### 2. Five-second exposure — the roof (3 min)

> Show the roof line for ~5 seconds, then remove it.

4. "What do you remember?"
5. "What do you think this product does — in your own words?" *(comprehension playback: score CLARITY)*
6. "Who do you think it's for?" *(score ICP ALIGNMENT)*

### 3. Pillar walkthrough (8 min)

> Show each pillar card, one at a time, competitor cards shuffled in blind.

For each card:
7. "Say this back to me like you're telling a friend." *(CLARITY)*
8. "Does this describe a problem you actually have? When did it last happen?" *(ICP ALIGNMENT + EMOTION — watch for the lean-in)*
9. "Do you believe it? What would you need to see to believe it?" *(proof demand — feed to the house's foundation)*

After all cards:
10. "Which one of these could only be about one specific product — and which could be anyone?" *(DIFFERENTIATION — if your card reads "anyone" and the competitor's reads "specific," that's the finding)*
11. "Rank them. Why that order?"

### 4. Emotion and urgency (4 min)

12. "Which line, if any, made you feel something? What?" *(EMOTION)*
13. "If this existed today, what would you do in the next 24 hours — honestly?" *(ACTIONABILITY — accept 'nothing' gracefully; it's data)*
14. "What would stop you?" *(objection harvest → persona matrix objection row)*

### 5. Close (2 min)

15. "If you had to describe this product in one sentence to someone in your situation, what would you say?" *(their sentence often beats yours — capture verbatim)*

### Scoring sheet (per participant × per stimulus, 1-5)

| Stimulus | ICP alignment | Differentiation | Clarity | Emotion | Actionability | Verbatim that justifies the scores |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Roof] | | | | | | |
| [Pillar 1] | | | | | | |
| [Pillar 2] | | | | | | |

**Decision:** ship ≥4.0 (no pillar <3.0, beachhead persona) · iterate 3.0-4.0 · reposition <3.0 on ICP/differentiation

## Filled example — Plotline

*Testing the Plotline house with the beachhead persona (Priya — PMs at 20–200-person B2B SaaS). n=6 per persona; competitor controls pulled verbatim 2026-06-12.*

**Stimulus cards (unbranded):**
- A. *Ask your product a question in plain English — get the answer in four minutes.* (roof, hero form)
- B. *Stop filing tickets to ask why trial users churn.* (pillar 2 compressed)
- C. *One definition of "activated" — every team, every answer, numbers that reconcile.* (pillar 3)
- D. *The complete analytics platform for data-driven enterprises* (competitor control — Vista hero, verbatim)
- E. *Dashboards your whole team will love* (competitor control — Pulse hero, verbatim)

**Warm-up probes, Plotline-specific:**
- "Walk me through the last time you needed to know why a metric moved. How long from question to answer?" *(if the answer is 'I checked a dashboard' — probe: 'and the follow-up why?' The ticket-and-wait story IS pillar 2's pain, attested live — and every stated wait time feeds the queue-wait estimate, which is currently self-reported from only 9 interviews.)*
- "Have product and marketing ever disagreed about the same metric? What happened in that meeting?" *(direct gap-closing question — the corpus still lacks a quote quantifying the reconciliation-argument cost; log every yes with their exact words)*

**Differentiation probe, Plotline-specific:**
- After ranking: "Card E says 'dashboards your whole team will love' — what tool is that? Could card B be the same tool?" *(if participants attribute B to Vista/Pulse, the wedge language isn't separating — rewrite before launch)*

**Example completed scoring row (participant P4, PM at an 80-person B2B SaaS):**

| Stimulus | ICP | Diff | Clarity | Emotion | Action | Verbatim |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A (roof) | 5 | 4 | 5 | 4 | 4 | "I have three of those tickets open right now. If it actually shows the query it ran, I'd trust it — I'd try it this week." |
| E (Pulse control) | 4 | 2 | 4 | 2 | 3 | "Every analytics tool says the team will love it. Nobody's team loves it." |

**Reading it:** A's clarity=5 with a spontaneous open-tickets story is the strongest possible signal — and note the differentiation control doing its job (E scores 2 on Diff: "that's all of them"). A's diff=4, not 5: two participants asked "isn't that what the big suites' new AI assistants do?" — exactly why the "only" claim stays gated on the rival AI-feature sweep.

**Decision rules applied to Plotline:** the "only analytics tool that…" phrasing is NOT in any stimulus — it's gated on the rival AI-feature sweep, and testing an unshippable claim wastes participants. "Ask anything" is excluded too (accuracy is verified only for the common question types). Test the demonstrable form ("get the answer in four minutes") instead.
