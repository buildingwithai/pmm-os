# Messaging Canvas Template

**Purpose:** The main organizing canvas for all product messaging — the working doc behind every message house, homepage, deck, and email. One canvas per product (or per major product line).

**When to use:** Immediately after positioning is complete (see the [positioning canvas](../../../positioning/assets/positioning-canvas-template.md)), and again at every quarterly messaging review. If someone asks "what's our message?" and the answer lives in six people's heads, you need this canvas today.

**Time to complete:** 2-4 hours with positioning + customer research in hand; do not attempt it without them.

**Not this template:** For the one-page artifact that circulates, use the [message house](message-house-template.md) — this canvas is the full reasoning behind it.

## Instructions

1. Complete the inputs section first — a canvas without positioning and voice-of-customer evidence is guessing, not messaging.
2. Write the primary message before the pillars. If you can't say the one thing, you have a positioning problem; go back one step.
3. Use customer language everywhere. Pull verbatim quotes from research; the customer's phrase beats your paraphrase.
4. Type every proof point: **fact** (source + date), **estimate** (method stated), or **assumption** (flagged). An untyped proof is a claim, not an argument.
5. Name your gaps explicitly. "No customer quote yet for pillar 3" is a workable canvas; a padded-over gap is a broken one.
6. Score the finished canvas with the [messaging audit checklist](messaging-audit-checklist.md) before circulating anything built on it.

## Filled example — Plotline

*Product: Plotline — self-serve product analytics (plain-English "Ask"). Research base: `demo/plotline-launch/` (beta telemetry, interview corpus, competitive fetches), desks dated 2026-06-12.*

### Inputs

**Positioning statement:**
> For product and growth teams at scaling B2B SaaS without a data team to lean on, Plotline is the self-serve product analytics tool that answers "why" in plain English in minutes — unlike Vista Analytics or a SQL queue, which make the people with the questions wait on the few who can write the query.

**Best-fit customer:** PMs and growth owners at 20–200-person B2B SaaS with real usage but no (or overloaded) data team ("Every 'why did this drop' is a ticket. The answer comes back in two days — the sprint's already planned" — PM, beta interview #7), sharpest beachhead: the PM who owns a metric and files tickets to understand it.

**Evidence base:** Competitive fetches of Vista Analytics + Pulse homepages and pricing (2026-06-12), G2 review mining of both, 14 beta-customer interviews + 6 win/loss calls (2026-05-10 → 2026-06-12), beta telemetry across 37 accounts.

**Claims NOT yet safe to ship:** "the only analytics tool where anyone can ask in plain English…" — gated on the rival AI-feature sweep (Vista's Copilot beta, Pulse's Insights AI, plus the LLM-analytics startups entering monthly). Pulse's Insights AI docs page 404'd, so "un-owned" is confirmed only against what Vista and Pulse ship today, not their betas. Owner: founding PMM; gate: ~1 week. Also gated: "ask anything" — Ask's accuracy is verified only for funnels, cohorts, retention, and segmentation; the claim stays scoped to those until the accuracy guardrail extends.

### The primary message

> **Ask your product a question in plain English — get the answer in four minutes.**

**Why this:** the plain-English self-serve Ask is the one capability neither fetched rival ships (Vista leads enterprise-suite power for analysts, Pulse leads dashboards — the "what," not the "why"; both route the follow-up question through someone who can build the query), and it lands on the ICP's exact native phrase — "everything is a ticket."

### Pillars

**Pillar 1: Ask in plain English**
- **Buyer pain:** "I know exactly what I want to know. I just can't write the query, so I file a ticket and wait." — PM, beta interview #7 (2026-06-04). The people with the questions can't get the answers.
- **Claim:** type the question — "where do trial users drop off before inviting a teammate?" — and Plotline returns the funnel, with the generated query and the metric definition shown under it.
- **Gain:** a 4-minute median time to first answer, vs. the ~2-day data-queue wait it replaces.
- **Proof points:**
  1. [fact] 4-minute median time to first answer — beta telemetry, n=37 accounts (2026-06-12).
  2. [fact] 71% of weekly active beta users write no SQL — beta telemetry (2026-06-12).
  3. [fact] Neither fetched rival ships plain-English self-serve: Vista's hero is "The complete analytics platform for data-driven enterprises" (analyst-first); Pulse's is "Dashboards your whole team will love" (homepage fetches, 2026-06-12).
  4. [assumption → close pre-launch] "PMs would rather type the question than learn a query builder" — inferred from beta behavior; no interview verbatim yet. Targeted question owned pre-launch.
- **Matters most to:** Priya (PM, beachhead); Marcus (lifecycle/PMM).

**Pillar 2: No tickets, no waiting**
- **Buyer pain:** "Every 'why did this metric move' becomes a ticket for the data team. By the time it comes back, the sprint's planned." — growth PM, win/loss call (2026-05-28). The ICP's native register is waiting and gut-decisions, not tooling.
- **Claim:** the auto-instrumented SDK captures events without an engineering ticket, and self-serve Ask removes the analyst from the loop for the common question — the queue drops out of the decision entirely.
- **Gain:** decisions made the same afternoon, on data — vs. a ~2-day wait or a call made on gut and relitigated next week.
- **Proof points:**
  1. [estimate] ~2-day median data-queue wait — self-reported across 9 of 14 beta interviews (method: median of stated waits).
  2. [fact] No engineering ticket to add an event — auto-instrumentation captures it (product demo).
  3. [fact] Vista implementations run weeks and assume a dedicated admin — their own onboarding docs + G2 reviews (fetched 2026-06-12).
- **Matters most to:** every persona; the emotional lead for launch copy (safer than the unverified "only" claim).

**Pillar 3: One set of numbers**
- **Buyer pain:** "We spent half the QBR arguing about whose activation number was right." — lifecycle marketer, win/loss call (2026-05-28). Definitions drift per team, per dashboard, per spreadsheet.
- **Claim:** Plotline's shared metric catalog defines "active," "activated," and "MTU" once — and every Ask answer uses those definitions, for every team.
- **Gain:** numbers that reconcile across product and marketing — the argument about whose number is right disappears from the meeting.
- **Proof points:**
  1. [fact] One definition per metric, enforced in every answer — the catalog is the source of truth (product demo).
  2. [fact] Every answer shows its metric definition and generated query — the transparency is checkable, not asserted (product demo).
  3. [assumption → close pre-launch] "Fewer reconciliation arguments" is unquantified — no customer quote yet puts a number on the QBR-argument cost. Targeted question added to the remaining beta interviews.
- **Matters most to:** Marcus (PMM); Dana (Head of Product, economic buyer).

### Objections and reframes

| Objection | Reframe |
|---|---|
| "We already have Vista." | "And how many of your team actually open it? Plotline is the one your PMs use without a data person." |
| "Plain-English analytics can't be trusted." | "Every answer shows the generated query and the metric definition. Transparent, not a black box — check it." |
| "We'll just ask our analyst." | "How long is that queue right now? Plotline frees the analyst for the modeling only they can do." |

### Channel expressions

- **Homepage hero:** *Ask your product a question. — The funnel, the cohort, the answer — in four minutes. No SQL, no ticket.*
- **Sales one-liner:** *"Plotline is the analytics tool where anyone on the team types a question in plain English and gets the answer themselves — in minutes, not a data-queue ticket."*
- **Cold-email opener:** *"How many product questions did your team not ask last month because the data queue was two days deep? Most PMs we interview stopped counting. Plotline answers the common ones in four minutes — and shows the query it ran."*
- **Social/native:** a genuine tactic write-up in r/ProductManagement's recurring "how do you get answers without a data team?" thread format (anchor: the 412-upvote tactics thread, 2026-05) — Plotline appears as the tactic, one link, not an ad read.

### Proof inventory and named gaps

**Strongest proof we own:** the live ask-to-answer demo — a question typed, the funnel back in minutes, the generated query visible under it. The whole argument in one screen recording.

**Gaps:**
- No named customer + quantified outcome for the case study — the coach review's launch gate; harvest from the beta cohort before Tier-1 marquee claims go live.
- "Only tool that…" unverified vs. the incumbents' AI betas — the rival AI-feature sweep gates the claim.
- The reconciliation-argument cost has no verbatim number — targeted interview question owned pre-launch.

### Governance

- **Owner:** founding PMM
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-12 (research date) · **Next review:** post-verification sweep, then quarterly
- **Consistency test:** pending — run the [message testing script](message-testing-script.md) internally first.
