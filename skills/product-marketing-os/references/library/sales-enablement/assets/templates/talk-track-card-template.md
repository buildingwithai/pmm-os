# Filled example — Talk-Track Card: Run the Wedge (Plotline plain-English Ask)

**Last Verified**: 2026-07-01
**Owner**: Founder (enablement owns, sales updates)
**Surfaced when**: First conversation · head of product at a 20–200-person B2B SaaS or a power-user PM · any mention of the data queue, waiting on answers, or shelfware analytics

---

## Objective

Get the prospect to experience (or commit to piloting) plain-English Ask — the type-a-question-get-the-funnel moment — within one week, measured by the 7-day first-real-answer activation metric.

## When to run it

- **Deal stage**: First call / first DM reply
- **Persona**: Head of product (validation motion) or a PM blocked on the data queue weekly (Team-upgrade motion)
- **Trigger signal**: Buyer says anything like *"we can't get answers out of our data,"* *"everything goes through one analyst,"* or names Vista/Pulse
- **Do NOT run when**: The conversation is about dashboards, BI, or Business-tier/SSO procurement — dashboards are commodity ground Plotline does not sell on, and the Business rung is explicitly deferred until the beta accuracy guardrail goes green. Redirect to the ask-a-question moment or escalate to the founder.

## Script (word-for-word, with variants)

**Know** (before you speak): (1) The queue is the why-now: the median question waits ~2 days for an answer, while Plotline's median time to a first answer is 4 minutes — and 71% of Plotline's active users write no SQL. (2) ⚠️ GATED CLAIM: never say "only analytics tool that answers plain-English questions" or "Ask is always right" — the accuracy-guardrail benchmark plus the rival AI-assistant sweep (Vista IQ, Pulse Copilot, the warehouse-native chatbots) has not completed. The sayable version is the category difference + live test below, with the generated query shown.

**Opener / hook** — pick one, verbatim:
1. "Quick question — of the last ten product decisions your team made, how many shipped before the data came back?"
2. "Everyone's building dashboards for last quarter's questions. Nobody's answering the question you have right now, at the moment you have it. That's the whole product."
3. *(colder read)* "You mentioned Pulse — it's great at showing what happened. We do the opposite job: answering the new 'why' nobody built a dashboard for."

**Core message** — 3 points, each mapped to a pillar, each with one proof:
1. **The moment, not the dashboard** *(pillar: plain English)* — "Type the question — 'why did trial conversion drop this week?' — and the funnel comes back, with the generated query and metric definition underneath. No ticket, no SQL." → Proof: 71% of active users write no SQL; every answer shows its query (live demo available).
2. **The queue broke decision speed** *(pillar: no waiting)* — "At today's pace, waiting isn't a discipline problem — it's arithmetic. A two-day queue means the roadmap call gets made on gut." → Proof: median ~2 days in the data queue vs 4 minutes median time to first answer in Plotline.
3. **Free where it counts** *(pillar: one set of numbers)* — "Ask is free to 10k tracked users with unlimited seats — Team only gates depth: the shared metric catalog, higher MTU, saved analyses." → Proof: usage-based MTU pricing; seats never billed — the strategy made visible.

**Show**: The two-minute live test — take a real question the team asked this week, first in their current setup, then typed into Plotline. End result first: the chart on screen with the generated query beneath it.

**Check question** — verbatim:
- "When was the last time you [one of your PMs] made a call on gut because the answer was still in the queue — and what did it cost?"

## Red flags — watch for, escalate, or kill

- **Prospect steers to dashboard/BI feature comparison** → do not follow; that ground is commoditized (Pulse sells it; free open-source dashboard tools exist). Reframe to the ask-a-question moment or end the play.
- **Prospect asks "so the AI is always right?" or "so you're the only one that does this?"** → do NOT confirm. "That's exactly what our accuracy guardrail and the rival sweep are verifying right now — what I can show you today is the live test, with the generated query on screen." Escalate to founder if a written claim is requested.
- **Prospect wants Business/SSO pricing on the spot** → deferred rung; park it: "Pricing an enterprise tier before the pilot proves the metric would be guessing — let's earn that conversation." Escalate.
